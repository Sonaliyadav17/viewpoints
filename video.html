"Startup Odyssey: Adventures, Sales, Family Triumphs"

During his Zomato journey, two crucial realizations unfolded. First, working in a startup is like doing an adventure sport at work—exciting, unpredictable, and involving risks. It demands energy, quick decision-making, and passion. Second, there's no strict job description in startups, making it inclusive for individuals with varying levels of experience.

After about a decade of work, he noticed that every job transforms into a form of sales. Professionals find themselves selling ideas, often with dramatic stories, to stakeholders for future funds. Even if monthly targets aren't met, storytelling becomes a tool to explain the circumstances. The pro tip he shared emphasizes how sales teaches the reality of consumers. It provides a direct insight into consumer needs and team performance, extending beyond merely meeting targets. Sales, he asserts, is instrumental in understanding and fulfilling consumer expectations effectively. Addressing the blue-collar attitude towards sales, contrasted with the white-collar perception of jobs from campus recruitment, involves looking at childhood memories and societal influences. Negative perceptions often stem from stereotypes of pushiness, associating salespeople with robbing individuals by selling costly items under the guise of business development. Overcoming these stereotypes involves highlighting the positive aspects of sales roles—helpfulness, expertise, and genuine care for customers' needs can reshape public perception. The wisdom of prioritizing family needs over societal opinions emerges as a cornerstone of success. While considering others' views is natural, making decisions based on what's best for family well-being and happiness is crucial. Different opinions may exist, but prioritizing family needs is paramount. A study revealing that 67% of CEOs have sales backgrounds underscores the importance of sales skills in leadership. This trend suggests that experiences gained in sales roles contribute to effective leadership, emphasizing the significance of diverse professional backgrounds in shaping successful CEOs and organizational strategies. The notion that everyone should learn sales at some point is emphasized. Learning sales skills in early adulthood, particularly around the age of 20, is considered advantageous. This age group typically possesses the energy and resilience needed to navigate the challenges associated with sales. Acquiring sales expertise early builds a strong foundation for a career and enhances adaptability for success in various life aspects.

moral- Embrace the adventurous nature of startups, recognizing that every job involves elements of salesmanship. Prioritize family well-being over societal opinions, understanding the universal applicability of sales skills. The study on CEOs reinforces the value of diverse skills in leadership. Success lies in adaptability, genuine connections, and a commitment to personal values.
